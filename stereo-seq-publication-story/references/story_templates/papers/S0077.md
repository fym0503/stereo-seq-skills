# S0077: Mouse Placentation Atlas

Metadata:
- Title: `A spatiotemporal transcriptomic atlas of mouse placentation.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Cell Discov, 2024, `10.1038/s41421-024-00740-6`
- Code: `https://github.com/BGIResearch/SAW`; `https://github.com/STOmics/Stereopy`; `https://github.com/BayraktarLab/cell2location`; `https://github.com/broadinstitute/Tangram`; `https://github.com/sqjin/CellChat`; `https://github.com/genostack/CellphoneDB`; `https://github.com/theislab/scvelo`; `https://github.com/aristoteleo/dynamo-release`; `https://github.com/aristoteleo/spateo-release`; `https://github.com/aertslab/pySCENIC`; `https://github.com/skvark/opencv-python`; `https://github.com/theislab/scanpy`

Story layer:
- Central question: How does placental spatial organization and cell-state regulation evolve across mouse placentation?
- Biological gap: Placental development involves multiple interacting compartments and stages that cannot be explained by dissociated cells alone.
- Technical gap: Requires time-resolved spatial sections, cell-type mapping, trajectory, LR and regulon analyses.
- One-sentence story: The paper builds a stage-resolved placental Stereo-seq atlas and uses it to explain trophoblast development, labyrinth signaling, imprinted gene programs, maternal-fetal interface regulation and high-fat-diet perturbation.
- Archetype: developmental_timecourse; regulatory_network.
- Final claim type: developmental atlas plus regulatory and perturbation hypotheses.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: timecourse atlas E7.5-E14.5, Louvain/manual subregions and spatial domain maps.
- Fig. 2: early trophoblast development, marker bubble plots, deconvolution, spatial maps and RNA velocity.
- Fig. 3: labyrinth development, temporal cell proportions, UCell vascular programs and LR communication.
- Fig. 4: imprinted gene spatial programs and Plagl1 GRN.
- Fig. 5: maternal-fetal interface layers, pathway scores, TF RAS and Atf3 GRN.
- Fig. 6: HFD perturbation, NNMF factors, spatial factor maps, enrichment and RT-qPCR validation.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 builds the developmental atlas. It lays out stages E7.5-E14.5, clusters bin50 data, manually annotates 24 placental/uterine subregions and displays them spatially across stages. Marker overlays and subregion proportions make the anatomy legible. For reuse, this is the required first figure for a timecourse tissue: show all stages in the same annotation language before asking lineage or mechanism questions.
- Fig. 2 zooms into early trophoblast development. It reclusters labyrinth/JZ bins from early stages, validates clusters with marker bubble plots and multicolor marker maps, deconvolves cell types, and uses RNA velocity/PAGA to infer direction in ectoplacental cone and TGC-related states. Hand1 expression and regulon activity connect trajectory to regulation. For reuse, only use velocity/trajectory when timepoints and lineage markers support direction.
- Fig. 3 focuses on labyrinth maturation. It quantifies temporal cell proportions, scores vascular gene programs with UCell, maps hormone/growth-factor genes, infers spatially restricted LR pairs and validates MDK with IHC and perturbation. The figure moves from developmental change to candidate signaling function. For reuse, a strong LR figure should include stage specificity, spatial expression and at least one orthogonal validation.
- Fig. 4 isolates imprinted genes as a special regulatory program. It spatially maps known imprinted genes, summarizes paternal/maternal imprinted gene scores by subregion and stage, then shows a Plagl1 regulon network. The story function is to identify a biologically themed gene program that follows placental compartments. For reuse, replace "imprinted genes" with any curated gene program that has strong domain relevance.
- Fig. 5 examines the maternal-fetal interface. It defines interface layers, tracks cell composition across stages, scores Hallmark programs, maps TF expression and regulon activity, builds an Atf3 network and summarizes decidua-JZ LR interactions. This is the interface-analysis template: first define the boundary, then compare cells, programs, TFs and communication across that boundary.
- Fig. 6 adds perturbation with high-fat diet. NNMF factors are learned in control/HFD interface data, selected factors are mapped spatially, enriched, linked to immune proportions and validated by RT-qPCR; Irf7 regulon analysis gives a regulatory angle. For reuse, use factor/module discovery for perturbation only when condition labels exist; otherwise the same plots should be called spatial programs, not perturbation effects.

Open method modules:
- `time_resolved_domain_annotation`: spatial_structure module; stage-specific subregion maps.
- `celltype_deconvolution_and_velocity`: trajectory module; maps reference cell types and infers directional flows.
- `spatial_lr_by_stage`: interaction module; spatially restricted LR pairs across development.
- `spatial_regulon_activity`: regulatory_network module; TF activity/RAS and GRN visualization.
- `perturbation_factor_programs`: differential_program module; NNMF factors for HFD versus control.

Reusable pattern:
- Timecourse papers can move from atlas -> key lineage/process -> signaling/regulon -> perturbation/validation.
- Use for new data when: there are ordered stages or perturbations and enough sections to support temporal direction.
- Do not transfer trajectory claims to single-stage data.
