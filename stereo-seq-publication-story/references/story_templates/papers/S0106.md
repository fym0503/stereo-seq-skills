# S0106: gsMap Complex Trait Mapping

Metadata:
- Title: `Spatially resolved mapping of cells associated with human complex traits.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Nature, 2025, `10.1038/s41586-025-08757-x`
- Code: `https://github.com/JianYang-Lab/gsMap`

Story layer:
- Central question: Can spatial transcriptomics identify where complex-trait genetic signals act in tissue?
- Biological gap: Trait associations are usually assigned to broad cell types, not spatially localized cell populations or microdomains.
- Technical gap: Requires an algorithm that combines spatial graph structure, expression specificity and GWAS SNP annotation.
- One-sentence story: gsMap learns spatial microdomains, converts spot/cell-specific gene specificity into SNP annotations, and maps human traits onto spatially localized cell populations across embryo, brain and primate cortex datasets.
- Archetype: method; cross_dataset_trait_mapping.
- Final claim type: method benchmark plus trait-to-spatial-cell association resource.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: explains gsMap: GNN embeddings, microdomain definition, gene specificity scores and SNP/trait association.
- Fig. 2: applies gsMap to mouse embryo, moving from tissue-level to single-cell epithelial spatial regions.
- Fig. 3: maps traits to adult mouse brain regions and cell types, including SCZ/Glu-neuron relationships.
- Fig. 4: zooms into CA1 Glu-neurons and spatial axes for SCZ-associated gradients and GO terms.
- Fig. 5: extends to macaque PFC and depression-associated Glu-neuron spatial patterns.
- Extended figures: simulations, human embryo, MERFISH replication, biological replicates, cross-species checks and drug target module scores.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 is a method schematic, not a biology result. It defines the data object, spatial graph, GNN embedding, microdomain neighborhood, gene specificity score, SNP annotation and S-LDSC trait-association test. The key story move is converting local spatial expression specificity into trait heritability enrichment. For reuse, all downstream claims depend on having GWAS summary statistics and a defensible SNP-to-gene mapping, not just a spatial object.
- Fig. 2 is the first biological demonstration in mouse embryo. It starts with tissue-level bin50 anatomy, maps multiple traits, then zooms into single-cell epithelial regions and correlates trait association with epithelial density. This shows that the method can move from whole-tissue association to a local cellular microdomain. For reuse, use a broad map first and a concrete spatial zoom second; otherwise method outputs can feel abstract.
- Fig. 3 transfers the demonstration to adult mouse brain. Region-trait heatmaps are compared with cell-type proportions, especially Glu-neuron abundance and schizophrenia association. The figure then maps trait significance at cell resolution in midbrain and hippocampal CA regions. For reuse, this is the pattern for asking whether a trait signal follows anatomical region, cell-type composition or a finer spatial axis.
- Fig. 4 is the mechanistic zoom-in. It defines hippocampal axes, maps SCZ association within CA1 Glu-neurons, correlates association with dorsal-ventral or deep-superficial positions, and performs GO enrichment on axis/trait-correlated genes. The claim is spatially patterned trait relevance, not simple cell-type enrichment. For reuse, this module requires a named anatomical axis and enough cells along that axis.
- Fig. 5 is the cross-species generalization. It applies gsMap to macaque PFC, maps depression association in Glu-neuron subtypes, tests lateral-medial gradients and compares gene enrichments to mouse brain patterns. The story function is robustness across species and datasets. For reuse, treat cross-dataset validation as a separate claim: same tool, independent tissue, related biological axis.
- Extended figures are essential for a methods paper. Simulations, biological replicates, MERFISH replication, human embryo analyses, cross-species comparisons and drug-target module scores show that the signal is not a one-off visualization. For reuse, a new method template should always reserve space for null controls, replicate consistency and at least two distinct case studies.

Open method modules:
- `spatial_gnn_microdomain`: method_modeling module; integrates expression, coordinates and optional cell-type priors.
- `gene_specificity_to_snp_annotation`: statistics module; maps spatial specificity to SNPs using TSS/SNP-gene linking.
- `trait_association_mapping`: cross_dataset_comparison module; computes spot/cell/region trait associations.
- `spatial_axis_correlation`: spatial statistics module; links trait association strength to anatomical axes.
- `replicate_and_cross_species_validation`: validation module; simulations, replicate correlations and human/macaque comparisons.

Reusable pattern:
- For methods papers, the story is method schematic -> simulation/null control -> multiple biological showcases -> replication/cross-species validation.
- Use for new data when: GWAS summary statistics, SNP-gene maps and robust spatial cell/domain annotations exist.
- Do not transfer to ordinary atlas analysis unless the question is trait/genetic association.
