# S0070: Medulloblastoma High Plasticity State

Metadata:
- Title: `High cellular plasticity state of medulloblastoma local recurrence and distant dissemination.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Cell Rep Med, 2025, `10.1016/j.xcrm.2024.101914`
- Code: `https://github.com/broadinstitute/inferCNV`; `https://github.com/dylkot/cNMF`; `https://github.com/theislab/paga`

Story layer:
- Central question: What malignant state drives medulloblastoma recurrence and dissemination, and can it be targeted?
- Biological gap: Progression from primary to local relapse and distant dissemination needed a cell-state explanation.
- Technical gap: Requires malignant-cell programs, spatial mapping, CNV/scATAC validation and perturbation experiments.
- One-sentence story: Recurrent/disseminated medulloblastoma contains high-cellular-plasticity malignant states marked by metaprograms and targetable genes, supported by spatial/genomic validation and functional assays.
- Archetype: tumor_progression; cell_state_mechanism.
- Final claim type: experimentally supported mechanism/target hypothesis.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: tumor ecosystem, molecular subgroup, GSVA pathways and survival marker context.
- Fig. 2: malignant populations across primary/local/disseminated samples, CNV, spatial mapping and FISH validation.
- Fig. 3: cNMF metaprograms identify high-plasticity clusters and markers, with IHC and survival association.
- Fig. 4: scATAC/chromatin accessibility supports distinct regulatory state.
- Fig. 5: PTPRZ1 mechanism and perturbation in cells.
- Fig. 6: animal model treatment validates anti-tumor effect.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 defines the medulloblastoma ecosystem and progression context. H&E displays normal, primary, invaded and disseminated tissue; t-SNE annotates malignant and non-malignant cells; subgroup/state plots separate SHH and G3 disease; GSVA and survival marker panels nominate clinically relevant programs. For reuse, tumor-progression stories should first separate molecular subtype and progression state so later markers are not confounded.
- Fig. 2 focuses on malignant populations. It separates primary/local/disseminated malignant cells, defines cycling/differentiated/quiescent states, shows marker feature plots, compares population proportions, validates Ki-67/NeuN by IHC, runs GO on progression DEGs, maps tumor populations spatially and includes chromosomal variation/CNV evidence. For reuse, malignant state claims are stronger when expression states are paired with CNV/genomic validation.
- Fig. 3 discovers high-cellular-plasticity states. cNMF metaprograms highlight clusters with mixed or plastic programs, scores and barplots quantify enrichment across progression, marker feature plots and IHC validate CENPF/PTPRZ1, early cerebellar precursor similarity contextualizes identity, survival analysis tests relevance, and DEG dotplots name candidate genes. For reuse, this is the state-discovery template: program factor -> cluster/state -> progression enrichment -> marker validation -> clinical relevance.
- Fig. 4 adds regulatory evidence from scATAC. It compares CENPF-positive and PTPRZ1-positive tumor nuclei with negative cells, tests HCP signature expression and gene activity, displays chromatin accessibility and nominates enriched TFs. The story function is to show that HCP is a regulatory state, not just an RNA cluster. For reuse, regulon/ATAC evidence should be used to support state stability, but not as proof of function by itself.
- Fig. 5 tests PTPRZ1 function in vitro. GSEA connects PTPRZ1-high cells to ECM/growth-factor receptor pathways, survival analysis supports clinical relevance, and siRNA/OE experiments test proliferation, differentiation markers and migration. This is where the paper moves from association to mechanism. For reuse, keep experimental perturbation separate from discovery figures so the evidence level is clear.
- Fig. 6 validates targeting in animal models. IHC localizes target markers in PDX lesions, MRI/H&E measures tumor burden, Ki-67/MMP9 stains read out proliferation/invasion and survival curves evaluate treatment effect. The figure elevates PTPRZ1/HCP from candidate marker to therapeutic hypothesis. For reuse, without animal or functional validation, stop at "candidate target" rather than "targetable mechanism."

Open method modules:
- `progression_state_atlas`: annotation module; primary/local/disseminated malignant states.
- `metaprogram_discovery`: differential_program module; cNMF/GSVA scores.
- `genomic_validation`: validation module; CNV and FISH.
- `chromatin_state_support`: regulatory_network module; scATAC accessibility and gene activity.
- `functional_target_validation`: validation module; siRNA/drug/PDX assays.

Reusable pattern:
- Strong mechanism papers go: state discovery -> spatial/genomic validation -> candidate marker/target -> perturbation.
- Use for new data when: disease progression labels and validation assays are available.
- Do not imply mechanism from spatial maps alone.
