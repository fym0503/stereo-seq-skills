# S0053: GF/SPF Cecum

Metadata:
- Title: `Effects of flora deficiency on the structure and function of the large intestine.`
- Journal/year/DOI: iScience, 2024, `10.1016/j.isci.2024.108941`
- Code: `https://github.com/1014723815/GF_SPF_cecum`; `https://github.com/OpenGene/fastp`; `https://github.com/tangrams/tangram`; `https://github.com/wu-yc/scMetabolism`

Story layer:
- Central question: How does absence of microbiota alter large-intestine morphology, cell composition and function?
- Biological gap: GF intestinal phenotypes needed spatially resolved mapping to cell types and anatomical layers.
- Technical gap: Requires combining gross morphology, scRNA, spatial domains, deconvolution and metabolomics.
- One-sentence story: GF mice show enlarged/thinned cecum and altered epithelial, mesenchymal and immune functions that are localized to spatial layers and supported by DEG/GO and metabolite changes.
- Archetype: condition_spatial_function; microbiome_effect.
- Final claim type: condition-associated spatial functional atlas.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: starts with phenotype: GF cecum morphology and wall thinning.
- Fig. 2: scRNA/ST overview, main cell types, DEG burden, BayesSpace domains and deconvolution.
- Fig. 3: mesenchymal subtypes, GO functions and GF/SPF spatial localization.
- Fig. 4: epithelial subtypes, Saa1-high enterocytes, columnar epithelial stratification and transport/nutrient genes.
- Fig. 5: immune subtype reduction, volcano DEG, GO and spatial immune distribution.
- Fig. 6: metabolomics links changed microbiota state to vitamins, amino acids, bile acids and other metabolites.
- Fig. 7: extends epithelial and fibroblast function patterns to colon.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 anchors the condition story in morphology. The paper shows GF versus SPF cecum gross anatomy, H&E wall thickness and quantitative weight/thickness tests. This gives the reader a visible phenotype before transcriptomics. For reuse, condition papers are easier to trust when they start from an observable phenotype, not only DEG tables.
- Fig. 2 builds the omics bridge. scRNA defines ten major cecum cell types, marker dotplots validate annotation, DEG counts nominate mesenchymal and immune cells as strongly affected, BayesSpace defines muscle/mixed/epithelial spatial layers, and deconvolution maps major cell types into GF/SPF tissue. The figure supports "which compartments changed and where." For reuse, pair condition DEG burden with spatial domain maps to decide which lineages deserve zoom-in figures.
- Fig. 3 zooms into mesenchyme. It defines mesenchymal subtypes, assigns subtype functions with GO, spatially localizes fibroblast and mesothelial subtypes in mucosal/submucosal/muscular layers, compares GF/SPF proportions and shows reduced structural programs. For reuse, this is a structure-building lineage module: subtype annotation -> layer localization -> condition proportion -> functional DEG/GO.
- Fig. 4 zooms into epithelium. It identifies epithelial subtypes, highlights increased Saa1-high enterocytes in GF cecum, maps Saa1 expression, shows columnar epithelial stratification and tests nutrient/water transport genes. The key story move is converting a subtype abundance shift into a spatially stratified functional explanation. For reuse, epithelial or layered tissues should include both subtype maps and axis/layer-aware functional genes.
- Fig. 5 zooms into immunity. It defines immune subtypes, reports reduction of mature NKT, IgA plasma and other immune cells, uses volcano/GO plots for functional impairment and deconvolves immune spatial distribution. The role of this figure is to tie microbiota loss to immune homeostasis. For reuse, keep immune claims proportional: show abundance, differential genes and spatial localization together.
- Fig. 6 connects spatial transcriptomics to metabolites. KEGG enrichment and metabolite boxplots show altered vitamins, amino acids, cortisol, bile acids and fatty acids, while SMC DEG/GO adds tissue-function context. This figure broadens the mechanism from cell programs to luminal metabolic state. For reuse, metabolomics should be treated as an external evidence layer; without it, do not claim metabolite mechanism from ST alone.
- Fig. 7 tests whether the pattern generalizes to colon. It maps colonic epithelial stratification, GO functions, epithelial proportions, nutrient transport genes, immune markers and fibroblast structural programs. The figure is a validation/extension module across related tissue sites. For reuse, use a second organ/region as extension after the primary tissue story is already coherent.

Open method modules:

Reusable pattern:
- Condition story: phenotype first, spatial layers second, cell type programs third, metabolite/functional explanation last.
- Use for new data when: condition groups, histology/morphology and spatial cell composition are available.
- Do not copy full pattern without phenotype/metabolome support; otherwise keep it as spatial cell-state condition analysis.
