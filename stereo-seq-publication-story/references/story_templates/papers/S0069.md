# S0069: Rectal Cancer NAC CAF Niche

Metadata:
- Title: `Cancer-associated fibroblasts undergoing neoadjuvant chemotherapy suppress rectal cancer revealed by single-cell and spatial transcriptomics.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Cell Rep Med, 2023, `10.1016/j.xcrm.2023.101231`
- Code: `https://github.com/BGIResearch/SAW`; `https://github.com/MarcElosua/SPOTlight`; `https://github.com/YuLab-SMU/clusterProfiler`; `https://github.com/satijalab/seurat`; `https://github.com/sqjin/CellChat`; `https://github.com/ventolab/CellphoneDB`; `https://github.com/saeyslab/nichenetr`; `https://github.com/broadinstitute/inferCNV`; `https://github.com/chris-mcginnis-ucsf/DoubletFinder`; `https://github.com/shiquan/PISA`; `https://github.com/yzf072/spaTrack`

Story layer:
- Central question: Which therapy-induced stromal states explain response or non-response to neoadjuvant chemotherapy?
- Biological gap: CAF heterogeneity and its spatial relation to immune/tumor programs during therapy response were unclear.
- Technical gap: Requires paired/sampled therapy conditions, scRNA subtyping, spatial localization and outcome association.
- One-sentence story: Therapy response is linked to distinct CAF states whose spatial colocalization with endothelial, immune and malignant programs explains suppressive or supportive tumor microenvironments.
- Archetype: disease_niche; therapy_response.
- Final claim type: therapy-response niche model with outcome association.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: scRNA atlas and treatment/outcome composition changes.
- Fig. 2: CAF subtype definition, markers, proportions, ST localization, GO and therapeutic index.
- Fig. 3: prostaglandin pathway links endothelial cells to pCAF/nCAF states with spatial colocalization.
- Fig. 4: CAF subsets modulate immune microenvironment, using cellular modules, pathway enrichment and spatial colocalization with immune states.
- Fig. 5: malignant expression programs, EMT DEG and correlations with CAF subsets.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 frames the therapy-response cohort. It shows the scRNA workflow, major tumor-infiltrating cell types from 333,285 cells, pre/post-therapy shifts, outcome-group differences and direction of mean proportion change. The role is to identify therapy as the main axis before focusing on CAFs. For reuse, response studies should show per-patient or per-sample composition changes, not only pooled UMAPs.
- Fig. 2 makes CAFs the focal population. It subclusters CAFs, validates markers with dotplots, compares CAF subset proportions across CR/PR/NR and PE/PO groups, maps CAF subsets in post-treatment ST samples, performs GO enrichment and defines a therapeutic index. This figure supports the claim that CAF states are response-associated and spatially localizable. For reuse, a stromal niche story needs subtype markers, outcome association and ST localization in the same figure block.
- Fig. 3 proposes endothelial prostaglandin signaling as a CAF regulator. GSEA identifies prostaglandin pathway enrichment in CAF subsets, EC subtype heatmaps show synthase expression, scatterplots connect EC_Vein abundance/synthase genes to CAF PG response, and spatial colocalization maps EC PG-synthase genes with CAF PG-receptor genes. For reuse, this is a good LR-plus-colocalization pattern: pathway score -> sender gene -> receiver gene -> spatial adjacency.
- Fig. 4 connects CAFs to immune state. A neighbor-joining tree groups TME cellular modules, ST colocalization counts immune cells near CAF subsets, pathway enrichment compares CD8 T cells across therapy/outcome groups, and ligand/gene correlations highlight CXCL12/SLIT2-related immune effects. The story function is to show that CAF states are embedded in immune-active or suppressive niches. For reuse, use distance-defined neighborhoods when claiming spatial modulation of immune cells.
- Fig. 5 links CAFs to malignant programs. NMF modules are built from malignant/healthy epithelial programs, module expression differs between NR and CR malignant cells, correlations connect malignant modules to CAF subset proportions, EMT DEGs are shown, and spatial proximity quantifies tumor cells around CAFs. This figure closes the loop from stromal state to tumor behavior. For reuse, avoid saying CAFs regulate tumor programs unless there is perturbation; with ST/scRNA alone, phrase as association or niche model.

Open method modules:
- `therapy_response_cell_composition`: statistics module; pre/post and CR/PR/NR composition shifts.
- `caf_state_definition`: annotation module; marker genes, subtype proportions and GO.
- `st_colocalization`: spatial_structure module; proximity/colocalization between CAF, EC, immune and tumor programs.
- `program_correlation`: differential_program module; GSEA/NMF programs correlated with cell proportions.
- `outcome_external_validation`: clinical_association module; TCGA/patient survival associations.

Reusable pattern:
- For cancer therapy data, define response-associated cell states, spatially map them, link them to immune/tumor programs, then validate with outcomes.
- Use for new data when: response labels or therapy timepoints exist.
- Do not transfer to single untreated tumor samples except as a descriptive niche analysis.
