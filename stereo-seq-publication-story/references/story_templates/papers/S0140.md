# S0140: Human Liver Progenitor Cells

Metadata:
- Title: `Integrative single-cell and spatial transcriptome analysis reveals heterogeneity of human liver progenitor cells.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Hepatol Commun, 2025, `10.1097/hc9.0000000000000662`
- Code: `https://github.com/BGIResearch/SAW`; `https://github.com/yzf072/spaTrack`

Story layer:
- Central question: What subtypes, spatial localization and state transitions define human liver progenitor cells across liver conditions?
- Biological gap: LPC heterogeneity and relationship to hepatocytes/cholangiocytes across healthy, fetal, cirrhotic and tumor contexts were unclear.
- Technical gap: Requires integrated scRNA, spatial localization, distance analysis, trajectory, DEG/enrichment and co-expression modules.
- One-sentence story: LPCs are identified in an integrated liver atlas, localized in Stereo-seq, split into subtypes and connected to condition-specific trajectories, programs and co-expression networks.
- Archetype: focal_cell_state_trajectory; spatial_localization.
- Final claim type: focal cell-state atlas and trajectory/program hypothesis.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: integrates scRNA from four conditions and annotates broad liver cell lineages.
- Fig. 2: focuses epithelial cells/LPCs, maps them to Stereo-seq slices and measures spatial distances to other cell types.
- Fig. 3: splits LPCs into subtypes, tests proportions, shows marker/pathway expression and Monocle pseudotime.
- Fig. 4: defines epithelial cellular modules, GO/DEG and trajectory stream among LPCs/cholangiocytes/hepatocytes.
- Fig. 5: condition-specific LPC signatures, module scores and GO/GSEA.
- Fig. 6: hdWGCNA co-expression modules and hub genes in LPC.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 is the broad liver atlas setup. It integrates scRNA from four liver conditions, annotates 15 major cell types, validates markers by heatmap and shows condition/sample composition. The figure creates a denominator for later LPC claims: LPCs are interpreted inside an epithelial and whole-liver context. For reuse, focal-cell papers should still begin with a broad atlas so the focal population is not floating outside the tissue ecosystem.
- Fig. 2 identifies where LPCs are spatially located. Epithelial cells are reclustered into cholangiocytes, hepatocytes and LPCs; cell-cycle effects are checked; classical markers validate identity; Stereo-seq slices are annotated; and distances from LPCs to other cell types are measured by cellbin centroids. The story function is spatial localization plus neighborhood context. For reuse, distance-to-cell-type analysis is a lightweight alternative to full CCI when the question is "where is this population positioned?"
- Fig. 3 defines LPC heterogeneity and pseudotime. LPCs are split into three subtypes, subtype proportions are compared, marker dotplots and pathway-expression panels characterize Wnt/Hippo/Notch-related biology, and Monocle orders LPCs across conditions. The figure supports a state-transition hypothesis, not a proven lineage. For reuse, trajectory language should be cautious unless conditions, marker gradients or validation support direction.
- Fig. 4 expands from LPCs to epithelial modules. It compares subcluster proportions, builds cellular modules by correlation, runs GO and DEG summaries for modules, and draws trajectory streamlines among LPCs, cholangiocytes and hepatocytes. This figure links a focal cell state to epithelial-system organization. For reuse, use module analysis when individual subtypes are too granular but coordinated programs are interpretable.
- Fig. 5 asks how LPC signatures differ by condition. It uses UMAP/pie charts for condition composition, marker expression panels, regeneration-related module scores, condition-specific DEGs, GO/GSEA and pathway summaries. The figure turns cell-state heterogeneity into disease/development context. For reuse, require multiple conditions or stages; with one condition, this becomes marker/program description only.
- Fig. 6 adds co-expression network structure. hdWGCNA modules are identified in LPCs, hub genes are displayed as networks, genes are embedded in UMAP-like network space, module eigengenes are compared by condition and pathway enrichment interprets module functions. For reuse, WGCNA-style modules are useful when enough cells/samples support co-expression; otherwise simpler marker/program scores are more defensible.

Open method modules:
- `integrated_condition_atlas`: annotation module; Harmony/Seurat integration across four liver conditions.
- `focal_population_spatial_mapping`: spatial_structure module; RCTD/spatial annotation and distance to other cell types.
- `subtype_and_pseudotime`: trajectory module; LPC subtype markers and Monocle pseudotime.
- `condition_specific_programs`: differential_program module; DEG, GO, GSEA and module scoring.
- `coexpression_network`: spatial_program module; hdWGCNA modules and hub genes.

Reusable pattern:
- If one population is the biological focus, use the atlas only as setup, then zoom into subtype -> spatial localization -> trajectory -> programs -> network.
- Use for new data when: a focal population is abundant and biologically motivated.
- Do not transfer trajectory direction without ordered conditions, known lineage markers or independent evidence.
