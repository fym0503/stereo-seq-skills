# S0097: Human Cerebral Cortex Atlas

Metadata:
- Title: `Charting the spatial transcriptome of the human cerebral cortex at single-cell resolution.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Nat Commun, 2025, `10.1038/s41467-025-62793-9`
- Code: `https://github.com/AllenInstitute/scrattch`; `https://github.com/BGIResearch/SAW`; `https://github.com/NeuroanatomyAndConnectivity/gradient_analysis`; `https://github.com/lcy1364/Cortex-Atlas-Code`; `https://github.com/neurostuff/NiMARE`

Story layer:
- Central question: How are human cortical cell subclasses spatially organized across regions and layers?
- Biological gap: Human cortical cell taxonomy needed single-cell-resolution spatial placement across many cortical areas.
- Technical gap: snRNA taxonomy lacks laminar and regional spatial arrangement; Stereo-seq provides cellbin-level tissue localization.
- One-sentence story: A large human cortex atlas maps known and refined neuronal subclasses into layers and cortical regions, revealing laminar gradients, regional specialization and local contact/proximity patterns.
- Archetype: spatial_atlas; anatomical_domain.
- Final claim type: descriptive atlas and region/layer organization resource.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: cohort/region sampling, snRNA/ST integration, subclass taxonomy and broad cortical atlas.
- Fig. 2: Stereo-seq segmentation, image+transcriptomic clustering, laminar annotation, marker validation and RCTD cell-type deconvolution.
- Fig. 3: glutamatergic subclass taxonomy and spatial gradients in example cortical regions.
- Fig. 4: region-specific distribution and functional enrichment of ET/IT neurons, with IF validation.
- Fig. 5: GABAergic subclass spatial organization and shallow/deep SST family patterns.
- Fig. 6: cell-cell soma contact/proximity patterns and laminar interaction heatmaps.
- Figs. 7-8: cross-region density/laminar quantification and refined V1/RORB layer 4 story.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 establishes the resource scale and coordinate system. It combines region sampling across 14 cortical areas, published BICCN taxonomy, new snRNA data and Stereo-seq layers. The dendrogram, UMAPs, barplots and spatial ridge/density summaries make the central claim that cortical cell subclasses can be placed across regions. For reuse, this is an atlas-entry template: define anatomical sampling first, harmonize cell taxonomy second, then show where the taxonomy lands in tissue.
- Fig. 2 is the laminar/domain foundation. The workflow uses H&E/ssDNA plus expression, DeepST-style spatial graph clustering, classical laminar markers and RCTD deconvolution. Marker spotplots and layer DEG/GO panels justify naming L1-WM domains. For reuse, this figure is the guardrail against freehand layer claims: layers should be supported by morphology, known marker genes and mapped cell-type composition.
- Fig. 3 focuses on glutamatergic neurons. UMAP taxonomizes excitatory subclasses, Stereo-seq maps them into cortical layers, and heatmaps/line charts show layer-dependent marker expression. The figure shifts from whole-cortex atlas to neuron subclass gradients. For reuse, pick one dominant lineage after domain annotation, then quantify spatial gradients rather than only showing colored maps.
- Fig. 4 turns subclass gradients into region-specific biology. It tests transcriptomic variation across regions, uses compositional analysis to identify changed subclasses, maps the relevant subclass in ACC/pain-related areas, and adds IF validation. RDA/biplots and enrichment panels connect cell subclasses to region functions. For reuse, this module needs multiple regions or replicates; otherwise it should be framed as descriptive localization.
- Fig. 5 gives the inhibitory-neuron counterpart. GABAergic subclasses, CGE/MGE-derived families, shallow versus deep SST families, DEG heatmaps and biological-process enrichment show that inhibitory classes also have laminar/regional structure. The story function is balance: atlas claims should not be only excitatory-neuron driven. For reuse, include both dominant and rarer lineages when they are biologically relevant and spatially resolvable.
- Fig. 6 uses segmented-cell coordinates for proximity rather than expression-only CCI. It shows soma contact/proximity enrichment among excitatory, inhibitory and non-neuronal subclasses, then summarizes laminar and regional interaction patterns with heatmaps and selected LR enrichment. For reuse, this is stronger than generic LR inference because it begins with physical neighborhoods; require cell segmentation or reliable cell centroids.
- Fig. 7 quantifies cross-region glutamatergic organization. It summarizes subclass density by cortical area, uses heatmaps/RDA to compare regional patterns and measures grey-matter layer arrangement across 44 slides. This figure converts visual atlas impressions into statistics. For reuse, use this after single-region examples, so readers can see whether examples generalize.
- Fig. 8 is a focused layer-4/V1 case study. It refines V1 laminar annotation, maps RORB-related glutamatergic structure, performs layer-4 DEG and GO analysis, and validates fine sublayers. The template is "zoom into one anatomical microdomain after the atlas is established." For reuse, choose a high-confidence domain with known markers and enough cells, not an arbitrary cluster.

Open method modules:
- `laminar_domain_annotation`: spatial_structure module; DeepST/spatial graph clustering plus laminar marker annotation.
- `reference_celltype_deconvolution`: annotation module; RCTD/spacexr and snRNA reference mapping.
- `subclass_density_gradient`: spatial statistics module; density maps, layer arrangement, RDA/biplot.
- `soma_contact_proximity`: spatial interaction module; contact/proximity enrichment among subclasses.
- `regional_functional_enrichment`: differential_program module; region/layer DEG and enrichment for selected subclasses.

Reusable pattern:
- For anatomy-rich tissue, organize the paper around “region/layer/domain first, cell subclass second, local interaction third”.
- Use for new data when: anatomical axes/layers can be annotated and there are enough sections/regions for comparison.
- Do not transfer without: reliable coordinate orientation, layer/domain annotation, and a reference or marker framework for cell subclasses.
