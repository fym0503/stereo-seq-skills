# S0178: P7 Mouse Brain Spatial Atlas

Metadata:
- Title: `A Cellular Resolution Spatial Transcriptomic Landscape of the Medial Structures in Postnatal Mouse Brain.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Front Cell Dev Biol, 2022, `10.3389/fcell.2022.878346`
- Code: `https://github.com/BGIResearch/SAW`

Story layer:
- Central question: Can Stereo-seq resolve postnatal mouse brain anatomical regions and cell types at cellular resolution?
- Biological gap: P7 mouse brain medial structures needed spatially resolved gene, region and cell-type maps.
- Technical gap: Requires high-resolution bin/cell segmentation, region clustering, marker validation and regulon analysis.
- One-sentence story: A P7 mouse brain Stereo-seq section is converted from QC maps into anatomical region annotation, marker-supported cell types and spatial regulon patterns.
- Archetype: spatial_atlas; anatomical_domain.
- Final claim type: descriptive single-section brain atlas.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: Stereo-seq workflow, molecular localization, ISH comparison, UMI/gene QC and replicate correlation.
- Fig. 2: bin50 spatial clustering, 41 anatomical regions, marker heatmap, region marker maps, layer DEG and predicted regulons.
- Fig. 3: segmented-cell UMAP and spatial map, 41 cell types, marker heatmap, scRNA correspondence and selected subtype spatial patterns.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 establishes data quality and spatial plausibility for a single P7 brain section. It shows the Stereo-seq workflow, analysis workflow, molecular localization of selected genes, spatial Sst/Gfap maps compared with Allen Brain Atlas ISH, UMI/gene maps at bin50 and replicate correlation. The story function is to convince the reader that transcript capture, spatial coordinates and known marker localization are trustworthy. For reuse on P7 h5ad data, make QC/spatial count maps and a small marker-validation panel before domain analysis.
- Fig. 2 is the anatomical-region figure. Unsupervised spatially constrained clustering at bin50 is manually annotated into 41 brain regions, then marker heatmaps and selected spatial marker maps justify the labels across olfactory bulb, cortex, hippocampus, striatum, hypothalamus, midbrain and cerebellum. Layer DEGs and predicted regulons add molecular structure within regions. For reuse, this is the closest template for a single-section brain dataset: annotation merge -> full spatial map -> region marker heatmap -> region-specific gene maps -> optional regulon/program map.
- Fig. 3 converts regional atlas to cell-type atlas. Segmented cells are visualized by UMAP and spatial coordinates, annotated into neuronal, glial, vascular, immune and epithelial populations, validated by marker heatmaps and compared with scRNA references. Selected subtypes are then shown spatially to interpret layered or compartment-specific localization. For reuse, if the input is already cellbin h5ad with annotation, use existing labels first, then quantify and visualize major cell classes within anatomical regions.
- Extended/supplemental logic is important for this paper even though the main story has only three figures. The missing checks to audit in full methods are segmentation quality, exact clustering resolution, marker list used for 41 anatomical labels, scRNA reference mapping strategy and pySCENIC/regulon parameters. For reuse, record these as audit fields before converting this paper into a formal skill behavior.

Open method modules:
- `stereo_qc_and_reference_marker_check`: preprocessing/validation module; UMI/gene maps, selected genes, ISH/ABA comparison.
- `anatomical_region_clustering`: spatial_structure module; spatially constrained clustering and manual annotation.
- `region_marker_deg`: differential_program module; marker heatmaps and region-specific genes.
- `cellbin_annotation`: annotation module; segmented-cell UMAP/spatial map and scRNA correspondence.
- `regulon_activity_map`: regulatory_network module; pySCENIC/regulon heatmap and spatial TF activity.

Reusable pattern:
- For single-section brain data, use a descriptive atlas story: QC -> anatomical domains -> marker support -> cell types -> optional regulons.
- This is directly transferable to the P7 mouse brain h5ad example.
- Do not force condition DEG, CCI or trajectory unless additional samples/timepoints exist.
