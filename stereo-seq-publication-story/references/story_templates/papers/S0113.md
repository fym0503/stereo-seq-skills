# S0113: PCOS Endometrium

Metadata:
- Title: `Single-cell profiling of the human endometrium in polycystic ovary syndrome.`
- Journal/year/DOI: Nat Med, 2025, `10.1038/s41591-025-03592-z`
- Code: `https://github.com/ReproductiveEndocrinologyMetabolism/Endo.R`

Story layer:
- Central question: Which endometrial cell states and interactions are altered in PCOS, and which changes are reversed by metformin or lifestyle intervention?
- Biological gap: PCOS endometrium pathology had not been resolved across epithelial, stromal and immune subclusters with spatial context.
- Technical gap: snRNA-seq alone gives subclusters but not their tissue localization; Stereo-seq adds spatial distribution and gene expression maps.
- One-sentence story: PCOS reshapes endometrial epithelial, stromal and immune states, and the paper links those changes to spatial expression, altered ligand-receptor crosstalk, treatment reversal and disease-associated traits.
- Archetype: disease_niche; intervention_response.
- Final claim type: condition-associated cell-state atlas with mechanism hypotheses and clinical/trait association support.

Figure logic:
- Fig. 1: establishes cohort, snRNA atlas, broad cell types and PCOS/control/intervention setup.
- Extended Data Fig. 2: validates Stereo-seq QC and label transfer, then shows spatial cell-type proportions and prediction scores.
- Fig. 2: epithelial subclusters, proportions, ESR1/steroid receptor expression, spatial placement and intervention effects.
- Fig. 3: stromal subclusters, proportions, hormone receptor/marker changes and intervention effects.
- Fig. 4: immune subclusters, DEG counts, top DEG dotplots and GO enrichment.
- Fig. 5: CellChat-derived epithelial-stromal-immune crosstalk, ligand/receptor dotplots and spatial expression of selected DEG/LR genes.
- Fig. 6: maps subclusters/DEGs to GWAS and clinical measures such as insulin resistance/hyperandrogenemia.

Detailed figure reading:
- Fig. 1 is the setup figure. It starts from patient groups and sampling phase, then builds a 247,791-nucleus snRNA atlas with seven broad endometrial lineages. The key move is not only annotation, but linking cell proportions to PCOS, metformin and lifestyle groups. This figure supports the claim that the disease/intervention comparison is grounded in a sufficiently broad cell atlas. For reuse, require sample-level condition labels and show UMAP, marker dotplot, per-sample composition and basic statistics before any spatial story.
- Extended Data Fig. 2 is the spatial credibility check. It shows adjacent H&E, genes per bin30, label-transfer annotation and prediction scores for control, PCOS and intervention samples. The useful template is: spatial QC map first, label map second, prediction/confidence map third, then bin-level cell-type proportions. For reuse, treat this as the required bridge between sc/snRNA states and spatial claims; if prediction scores are weak, downstream spatial interpretation should be downgraded.
- Fig. 2 zooms into epithelial cells. It subclusters epithelial nuclei, defines markers by heatmap, compares subcluster proportions across groups, tests ESR1 and DEG shifts, and spatially/IF-validates selected epithelial signals. The story function is to turn a broad epithelial change into named epithelial states and hormone-response programs. For reuse, this is the pattern for a major lineage: subcluster -> markers -> proportions -> key receptor/gene validation -> DEG/GO.
- Fig. 3 repeats the same logic for stroma. Stromal subclusters are defined by UMAP and marker heatmap, then compared across control, PCOS and interventions. The paper uses DEG counts, top DEG dotplots and GO heatmaps to argue that PCOS alters stromal programs and interventions partially reverse them. For reuse, keep the repeated structure across lineages because it makes the disease story comparable instead of anecdotal.
- Fig. 4 does the immune/endothelial compartment. It uses subcluster UMAPs, marker heatmaps, proportions, DEG dotplots and GO enrichment to identify which immune programs differ in PCOS and which are intervention responsive. This figure mainly supports cell-state/function claims, not spatial causality. For reuse, do not over-interpret low-abundance immune subclusters unless counts per group and per sample are visible.
- Fig. 5 is the mechanism-hypothesis figure. CellChat predicts epithelial-stromal-immune ligand-receptor pathways, dotplots show expression of ligands and receptors, heatmaps show treatment-reversed LR genes, and spatial expression panels localize selected genes. The story function is to move from "cell states change" to "these compartments may communicate differently." For reuse, run CCI only after subclusters and DEGs are stable, and always spatially display the selected LR genes.
- Fig. 6 is the clinical/trait relevance figure. CELLECT links cell subclusters to GWAS traits, and DEG expression is correlated with insulin resistance and androgen measures. This figure does not prove mechanism; it prioritizes disease-relevant cell states and genes. For reuse, only use this module when clinical phenotypes, GWAS resources or trait-linked gene sets are available.

Open method modules:
- `cohort_snRNA_atlas`: annotation module; UMAP, marker dotplot, proportions; supports “which cell states exist”.
- `stereo_label_transfer_qc`: spatial validation module; H&E, genes per bin30, UMAP-label transfer and prediction score maps; supports “snRNA states can be spatially localized”.
- `condition_deg_go_by_subcluster`: differential program module; MAST/Seurat DEG and GO heatmaps; supports “PCOS changes cell-type programs”.
- `cellchat_crosstalk`: interaction module; CellChat networks, heatmaps and LR dotplots; supports “altered cell-cell communication”.
- `trait_and_clinical_link`: clinical association module; GWAS/CELLECT and DEG-clinical correlations; supports disease relevance.

Reusable pattern:
- Build a matched single-cell/spatial disease atlas, split major lineages into subclusters, test condition/intervention changes, spatially validate key genes, and use CCI only after subclusters are biologically interpretable.
- Use for new data when: disease/control or treatment groups exist, with enough samples to compare subcluster proportions and DEG.
- Do not transfer without: condition labels, intervention/clinical metadata, or a defensible cell-state annotation.
