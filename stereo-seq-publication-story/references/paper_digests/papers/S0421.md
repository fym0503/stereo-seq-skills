Figure-By-Figure Caption Digest:
- Figure 1: The schematic overview of the TriCLFF framework. The TriCLFF framework takes (a) spatially paired gene expression and image patches as input and generates integrated embeddings as output for each spot. Specifically, the input data are augmented and then fed into three encoders (GATE, MLP encoder, and Swin-Transformer encoder) to extract embeddings for (b) spatial associations, (c) gene expression levels and (d) histological morphology features, respectively. The embedding for each modality is obtained through reconstruction loss and six intra- and inter-modality contrastive learning losses. (e) The final low-dimensional latent embeddings are further integrated using a weighted sum method and subsequently used for (f) spatial domain identification.
