Figure-By-Figure Caption Digest:
- Fig. 1: Overview of STAGATE. STAGATE first constructs a spatial neighbor network (SNN) based on a pre-defined radius, and another optional one in the dashed box for 10x Visium data by pruning it according to the pre-clustering of gene expressions to better characterize the spatial similarity at the boundary of spatial domains. STAGATE further learns low-dimensional latent representations with both spatial information and gene expressions via a graph attention auto-encoder. The input of the auto-encoder is the normalized expression matrix, and the graph attention layer is adopted in the middle of the encoder and decoder. The output of STAGATE can be applied for identifying spatial domains, data denoising, and extracting 3D spatial domains.
