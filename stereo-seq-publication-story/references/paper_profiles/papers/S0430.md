Figure-By-Figure Caption Digest:
- Figure 1:: An overview of the SGAE framework. The SGAE algorithm consists of 3 key modules. First, the graph distortion module generates 2 distorted graphs by introducing both attribute and graph disturbances. Second, the encoder module generates 2 sets of representations for each sample. Third, the redundant reduction module ensures that the same sample within the 2 distorted graphs has identical representations at both the feature and sample levels. Last, the discriminative representations are applied to clustering algorithms such as K-means to decipher spatial domains.
