- Figure 1: QR‐SIDE is able to infer the cell composition for each multi‐cellular spatial localization. QR‐SIDE only requires spatial transcriptomic data associated with localization information and cell‐type specific marker gene list. With these inputs, QR‐SIDE initializes the cell‐type composition using latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA) and subsequently clusters the initial composition using the Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) to obtain the initial topic assignment. QR‐SIDE proceeds to separately model the marker/non‐marker gene expression using a mixture Poisson regression model and a Poisson factor model. The spatial localization is then incorporated into QR‐SIDE through a Potts model. In particular, QR‐SIDE can evaluate the spatial heterogeneity of individual marker genes, and adjust their contribution to deconvolution.
