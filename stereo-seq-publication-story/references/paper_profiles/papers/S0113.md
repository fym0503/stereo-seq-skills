The key move is not only annotation, but linking cell proportions to PCOS, metformin and lifestyle groups. This figure supports the claim that the disease/intervention comparison is grounded in a sufficiently broad cell atlas. For reuse, require sample-level condition labels and show UMAP, marker dotplot, per-sample composition and basic statistics before any spatial story.
- Extended Data Fig. 2 is the spatial credibility check. It shows adjacent H&E, genes per bin30, label-transfer annotation and prediction scores for control, PCOS and intervention samples. The useful template is: spatial QC map first, label map second, prediction/confidence map third, then bin-level cell-type proportions. For reuse, treat this as the required bridge between sc/snRNA states and spatial claims; if prediction scores are weak, downstream spatial interpretation should be downgraded.
- Fig. 2 zooms into epithelial cells. It subclusters epithelial nuclei, defines markers by heatmap, compares subcluster proportions across groups, tests ESR1 and DEG shifts, and spatially/IF-validates selected epithelial signals. The story function is to turn a broad epithelial change into named epithelial states and hormone-response programs. For reuse, this is the pattern for a major lineage: subcluster -> markers -> proportions -> key receptor/gene validation -> DEG/GO.
- Fig. 3 repeats the same logic for stroma. Stromal subclusters are defined by UMAP and marker heatmap, then compared across control, PCOS and interventions. The paper uses DEG counts, top DEG dotplots and GO heatmaps to argue that PCOS alters stromal programs and interventions partially reverse them. For reuse, keep the repeated structure across lineages because it makes the disease story comparable instead of anecdotal.
- Fig. 4 does the immune/endothelial compartment. It uses subcluster UMAPs, marker heatmaps, proportions, DEG dotplots and GO enrichment to identify which immune programs differ in PCOS and which are intervention responsive. This figure mainly supports cell-state/function claims, not spatial causality. For reuse, do not over-interpret low-abundance immune subclusters unless counts per group and per sample are visible.
- Fig. 5 is the mechanism-hypothesis figure. CellChat predicts epithelial-stromal-immune ligand-receptor pathways, dotplots show expression of ligands and receptors, heatmaps show treatment-reversed LR genes, and spatial expression panels localize selected genes. The story function is to move from "cell states change" to "these compartments may communicate differently." For reuse, run CCI only after subclusters and DEGs are stable, and always spatially display the selected LR genes.
- Fig. 6 is the clinical/trait relevance figure. CELLECT links cell subclusters to GWAS traits, and DEG expression is correlated with insulin resistance and androgen measures. This figure does not prove mechanism; it prioritizes disease-relevant cell states and genes. For reuse, only use this module when clinical phenotypes, GWAS resources or trait-linked gene sets are available.
